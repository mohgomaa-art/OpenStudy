Pathophysiology of Acute Kidney Injury

Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) is a complex syndrome characterized by a rapid decline in kidney function, leading to the accumulation of waste products and dysregulation of fluid and electrolyte balance.

Explore the three main types of AKI below to understand their specific mechanisms and causes.

Prerenal AKI

Inadequate blood flow to the kidneys.

Intrinsic AKI

Direct damage to kidney tissue.

Postrenal AKI

Obstruction of urine outflow.
